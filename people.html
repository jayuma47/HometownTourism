CULTURE

The residents take a lot of pride of Kyoto's rich history as the former capital of Japan. Because of its long history, they work to preserve the city's arts, craft, and customs. The locals here are known for their politeness, they use formal attitude when it comes to social interactions. The city is a hub for arts and craft, and most of the locals practice this tradition and can be passed down to future generations. Residents also often joins in local festivals and events, having a strong sense of community.

HISTORY

The city was once home to aristocrats and scholars, with works like the Tale of Genji being created here. During the feudal period, power shifted to the military, but Kyoto remained an important cultural center. The rise of samurai culture influenced the city, and magnificent temples and gardens were built. In the Muromachi period, the city saw the establishment of the Ashikaga shogunate, further cementing its cultural significance.
